Welcome to ESDC!

ESDC, the fourth-largest department within the Government of Canada, has four ministers and about 36,000 employees who work to improve the standard of living and quality of life for all Canadians by supporting the development of a highly skilled labour force and promoting an effcient and inclusive labour market. ESDC supports and is supported by the following portfolios:

Employment and Social Development

They are responsible for developing, managing and delivering social programs and services. This includes consultations, frameworks, plans and research.

Read more about the portfolios and programs

Labour Program

They protect the rights and well-being of both workers and employers in federally regulated workplaces. It works closely with provincial and territorial governments, unions, employers, international partners, and other stakeholders to promote fair, safe and productive workplaces and collaborative workplace relations.

Service Canada

They provide Canadians with a single point of access to a wide range of government services and benefits. It seeks to improve services for Canadians by working with partners to provide access to the full range of government services and benefits that Canadians want and need through the Internet, by telephone, in person or by mail.

Read about our Leaders

Government departments are headed by ministers, who receive confidential advice from the public service and are held accountable for their decisions in Parliament and the country.

Deputy ministers are senior civil servants who take political direction from the minister and are responsible for the department’s day-to-day operations, budget and program development.

Pre-Arrival Checklist

  • Stay in regular contact with your manager, especially if there is a long period between the job offer and the start date.
  • It may be too early to know if you need workplace accommodations, but take the time to speak to your new manager about the workplace and any potential workplace accommodations
  • Ensure your manager sends you reference materials (i.e. phone list, organizational chart with titles, staff lists, other contact lists, voicemail and email instructions, job description, etc.)
  • You should receive your letter of offer three weeks before your start date.
  • Before your first day, make sure you are clear about the location, contact information for your manager and/or supervisor and/or administrative support staff for your new team and the date and expected time of your arrival.

Important

For current public servants joining ESDC from another department within the Public Service of Canada, please ensure you bring a copy of your leave balance (preferably on the last day worked in your previous job) and your “MyKey”. This will allow you to access encrypted files, decrypting files, emails, and for accessing your pay information via the Compensation Web Access (CWA) website
